  🗣️  THE 60-SECOND ELEVATOR PITCH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Every company using cloud — Amazon, Netflix, Uber — faces the same problem:
how many servers should I run right now? Too many servers? You're burning
money. Too few? Your website crashes and customers leave.

Today, companies use simple rule-based systems that are always reactive —
they scale up AFTER the crash has already started. Our project uses AI
(Reinforcement Learning) to build an auto-scaler that predicts traffic spikes
and scales up BEFORE they happen — like a self-driving car for your cloud
infrastructure.

We built a full simulation environment where AI agents learn to make scaling
decisions, and we grade them on a 0-1 score. Our AI agent scores 80% —
beating the industry-standard approach by 20%."

  Friday night, 200 orders come in. You only have 3 ovens running. By the

OUR SOLUTION:
  An AI that watches order patterns and turns on ovens at 5pm — before the
  rush hits at 7pm. It also turns them off at midnight when orders stop.
  Perfect balance between fast service and low electricity bills.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ❓  QUESTIONS JUDGES MIGHT ASK (& YOUR ANSWERS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "How is this different from Kubernetes HPA?"
───────────────────────────────────────────────
  "HPA is reactive — it waits until CPU crosses a threshold, then scales.
  Our agent is predictive — it reads traffic trends and scales BEFORE the
  spike. It also uses multi-objective optimization balancing cost, latency,
  SLA, and stability simultaneously."


Q: "Why reinforcement learning instead of a simple formula?"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  "Traffic patterns are non-stationary — they change over time (flash
  sales, growth, weekend patterns). A fixed formula can't adapt. RL agents
  learn to generalize across different patterns and optimize multiple
  competing objectives simultaneously."


Q: "Can this work in production?"
──────────────────────────────────
  "Yes! Our environment mirrors real infrastructure (warm-up delays,
  exponential latency, cost models). The observation space matches what
  Prometheus/CloudWatch provides. The action space maps directly to
  Kubernetes API calls. The trained policy can be deployed as a custom
  HPA controller."


Q: "What's the observation space?"
───────────────────────────────────
  "11 dimensions: timestep, incoming traffic, active servers, warming
  servers, CPU load, latency, dropped requests, served requests,
  cumulative cost, traffic trend, and normalized time-of-day. This gives
  the agent full situational awareness."


Q: "Why only 5 discrete actions?"
──────────────────────────────────
  "We chose discrete over continuous for training stability with DQN/PPO.
  The 5 actions (+3, +1, 0, -1, -3) cover all scaling scenarios:
  aggressive/cautious scale-up, hold, cautious/aggressive scale-down."


Q: "What's the hardest part?"
──────────────────────────────
  "The reward engineering. If you make cost penalty too high, the agent
  under-provisions and drops requests. If you make drop penalty too high,
  it over-provisions and wastes money. We spent significant effort
  balancing the 5 reward components to produce agents that find the
  sweet spot."
